Aerospike Nozzle

In Fall 2021, a team of 4 from the Boston University Rocket Propulsion Group including myself proposed the independent project to test an aerospike nozzle for our two-semester senior design capstone course. Using the experience we accumulated from BURPG, we plan to test a variety of test stand designs to achieve thrust vector control through flow control; doing so would greatly increase the viability of aerospike engines in industry. To simplify the cost of the student project, we plan to use inert gas flows to simulate propellants and 3D printing the nozzle. Having the most fluids experience from BURPG and internships, I am serving as the lead fluids engineer to design the test stand for the thruster. We are currently researching aerospike nozzle designs to size a thruster given our limitations and designing the thruster test stand.

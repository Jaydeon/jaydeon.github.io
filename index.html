About Me

I'm a 20 year old student living in Boston and New York. Born in New York City to my immigrant parents from Hong Kong, I'm a first-generation college student attending Boston University in Massachusetts to study mechanical and aerospace engineering. After I graduate in Spring 2022, I plan to pursue my passion for literal rocket science.

"Mankind was born on Earthâ€” it was never meant to die here." Sad as it is to imagine, what happens when Earth is no longer sustainable? Our future is in interstellar exploration, and it's a dream for me to be able to use my experience to develop modern launch vehicle technology as the NewSpace industry emerges.

As a child I was enamored with reading about the universe and drawing from my overactive imagination, and as a teenager I made a hobby out of building paper crafts and designing model rockets. At different points in my youth, I thought I wanted to be an auto mechanic or an artist or an astronomer. It took me sixteen years to realize that technology, art, and astronomy converges into aerospace engineering. Now as an adult I work on propulsion engines in industry and build liquid rockets with BURPG. Subconsciously when I was young and actively now, my life has always pointed towards a calling in rocket engineering.

Outside of engineering though, my interests include playing and listening to music, drawing art and designing graphics, and night sky watching for constellations and meteor showers. I stay active by running around the cities I explore, hiking home in the Hudson Valley, and playing basketball with my friends. Traveling is another outlet of my curiosity for exploration, and with my travels comes an adventure to seek out the best food in every city. I love learning more about the world through new experiences, and using these experiences to mentor and help others around me.
